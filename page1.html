Engine Air and Cabin Air Filters

One of the top four common maintenance is engine and cabin air filters. It is also a relatively easy one to do at home, but be careful!

Good Bad Fun Facts
Saves money, is a relatively easy process on the majority of cars For many newer cars the clips around the engine airbox have been replaced with elusive or proprietary screws and no longer the traditional clips, can cause you to spend money on buying unneccasary tools My 2008 Toyota Crown Athlete, had an extra Air Purifier Filter, which is found on high-end cars such as S-Class Mercedes, Rolls-Royces, and other vehicles of the sort
It is also a common maintenance item on all cars and has a frequent change rate, helps to learn so you do not have to make too many trips to the shop With age plastics can get brittle and start cracking if not removed carefully There are different types of cabin air filters, regular ones, carbon, electrostatic, and multilayer, each with their own advantages
Can be fun to do, also gives you peace of mind as you know, that you and your engine are breathing healthy air PLacement of filters in reverse order is another issues, many filters have a designated arrow to showcase which way to place them and from personal experience I have seen many upside down filters, this can cause suffocation, for cabin it can make the blower motors work harder, causing increased wear and for engine it can cause lack of air, which messes up many things Changing a dirty air filter can increase HP of a car by a couple units and help the engine last longer
